# Extra Hours, Lost in Smoke

**Format:** Living Comic (6 scenes)

---

## Scene 1 — wide

A neighborhood park at late afternoon: the sun is still high enough to be annoying, yet the light looks filtered through dirty glass. People linger on benches, all looking upward like they expected a better performance from the day.

- "We get a few extra hours of sun and somehow the sky still comes with a disclaimer."
- "I was promised summer. This feels like a beta version."

## Scene 2 — medium

On a sidewalk, the protagonist stands beside a closed lemonade cart, checking the time as if daylight can be negotiated back by schedule.

- "It’s still summer daylight, technically."
- "Technically is doing a lot of work here."

## Scene 3 — over-the-shoulder

From behind the protagonist, a rooftop view shows the sun dropping behind a curtain of smoke. A neighbor points with the kind of energy usually reserved for bad news and potholes.

- "We’d get another hour if the sky wasn’t renting space to smoke."
- "That’s not a sunset. That’s a warning label."

## Scene 4 — close-up

The protagonist’s hand hovers over a windowsill thermometer, then points out the light falling across a houseplant that has clearly also noticed the problem.

- "Look at that. Even the plant is getting shortchanged."
- "We’re all just losing our bonus sunlight to bureaucracy and smoke."

## Scene 5 — whiteboard

A community room whiteboard lists casual summer plans that now read like abandoned ambitions: long walks, porch sitting, and ‘maybe sunset.’ Someone has crossed out ‘clear sky’ and replaced it with ‘air quality pending.’

- "We were supposed to have those long golden evenings."
- "Now we have long gray evenings, which is not the same genre."

## Scene 6 — medium

Back outside at dusk, the protagonist and a few neighbors stand in the half-light, not exactly hopeful, but stubbornly present. The sun is nearly gone behind the smoke, yet the group keeps looking west as if collective annoyance might improve atmospheric policy.

- "Well. That was our extra hour."
- "Nature really said: best I can do is grievance."
