# lobot-tui: The Terminal That Notices Everything

**Format:** Living Comic (9 scenes)

---

## Scene 1 — wide

A dim control room shows the Lobot cluster dashboard glowing on a large terminal while the operator stands center-frame, taking in pods, nodes, and resource groups at a glance. The interface looks calm in the way only something barely holding together can look calm.

- “Everything is green, which is either excellent or a very specific kind of warning.”

## Scene 2 — medium

The operator leans toward a resource-group table, comparing CPU, RAM, and GPU allocation for jupyter workloads with the concentration of a surgeon and the speed of someone who has seen quota graphs before.

- “If the GPU bar is honest, the cluster is at least pretending to be sane.”

## Scene 3 — over-the-shoulder

From behind the operator, the node allocation table expands to reveal per-node CPU, RAM, GPU, and Longhorn disk usage. One node is politely unavailable in the exact way a parking cone is unavailable.

- “Cordon status: the cluster has entered its brief and voluntary phase of not trusting that node.”

## Scene 4 — close-up

A tight shot on streaming pod logs shows scrolling lines, stack traces, and one line of text that looks almost apologetic. The operator has opened a shell into the pod, which is apparently how one asks a container to explain itself.

- “There it is. The error message that came with feelings.”

## Scene 5 — terminal

The operator initiates a node drain and pauses at the double-keypress confirmation. The UI insists on certainty; the operator, after a beat, grants it. Somewhere deep in the cluster, a workload receives its notice politely and without drama.

- “This is the part where the interface checks whether I really meant it. I did, unfortunately.”

## Scene 6 — whiteboard

A whiteboard beside the terminal shows a hand-drawn rollout plan for image-pull, image-cleanup, and apply-config operations. The annotations are neat until they become honest, then they become slightly sarcastic.

- “We call it a runbook because ‘evidence of past pain’ was too long for the header.”

## Scene 7 — medium

Back at the dashboard, the operator watches the tables settle after maintenance. Pod phases update, node status changes, and the resource bars slide into a more believable arrangement. The system has not become perfect, only measurable, which is often enough.

- “There. Not fixed, exactly. But now it’s the kind of broken we can schedule.”

## Scene 8 — hallway

The operator steps into a quiet hallway, listening to the cooling fans through the wall like an extremely disciplined weather report. The recovery has shifted from crisis to watchfulness, which is the administrative equivalent of a victory lap with paperwork.

- “No alerts is not a philosophy, but it does improve posture.”

## Scene 9 — wide

The final shot returns to the control room. The dashboard glows steadily, the operator has sat back a little, and the cluster appears operational in the exact way a well-run machine looks: constantly, quietly negotiable.

- “All common admin operations available. That phrase should be on a monument somewhere, preferably one with backups.”
